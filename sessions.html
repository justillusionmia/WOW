E's of road safety:
1. Education

-Passages

Steps to cross the street:
1. Stop, Look, Listen, Live
2. Make eye contact with the driver, the driver should be able to see you, you should be able to see the driver.
3. Make sure the vehicle has come to a complete on either side of the road
4. Look right then left then right again

You are supposed to cross on the pedestrian crosswalk
You are not supposed to cross between parked vehicles
You are not supposed to cross around a bend

12 years and under:
Back seat because of size, height and age
5 years and under:
Back seat in a booster or car seat

Do not ride bikes on:
Pavement
Highway
Main road
Or without breaks

Session Seven

Objective: First Aid and CPR

CPR- Cardo pulmonary resuscitation
*unconscious not breathing is when someone needs CPR

1. Recognize someone is sick
2. Ask for help
3. Put on safety gloves (protect yourself)

On an adult:
Look, listen and feel for breathing for 10 seconds
For 18 seconds do 30 chest compression
Push down 2 cm
2 mouth to mouth breathing

On a child:
Use one hand
20 times for 18 seconds
Push down 1 cm
1 breath mouth to mouth

Reflection:

One of the most helpful seminars we've had so far was this one on first aid and CPR training. Someone's life can be seriously changed by knowing how to react in an emergency. The real-world demonstrartion reinforced the techniques we learned and made them easier to remember. I now feel more confident in my ability to help others in challenging situations because of this session.

Objective: Gender, Relationships, and Sexual Health

Determinants of biological sex:
-binary (male or female)
-chromosomal/genetic XX vs XY
-internal reproductive anatomy

Gender
Gender and sex are no longer considered to be interchangeable terms

Gender refers to the cultural and social distinctions associated with being male or female

Gender identity, an individuals self conception as being male or female as distinguished from actual biological sex. For most persons, gender identity and biological characteristics are the same

Sexual orientation is about who you're attracted to and want to have relationships with. Sexual orientations include heterosexual (straight), homosexual (gay, lesbian), bisexual and asexual.

Barriers to responsible adolescent sexual behaviour:
-Nobody talks to you about sex/sexuality
-You do not know where to get information or help
-You do not have access to family planning and or birth control
-You get your “facts” from “pop culture” and pornography

Practices for a good sexual health:
-Understand the laws, religious guidelines and cultural norma that apply to sexual relationships
-Know the risks involved with sexual intercourse and other sexual acts
-Protect yourself from harm or incarceration
